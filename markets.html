Platform Markets

Primary & Secondary Market Structure

Crowdaxe operates two distinct marketplaces — a primary market for initial investment placement and a secondary market for liquidity through structured position transfer.

Primary Market

New Investment Placements

The primary market is where approved issuers introduce new fundraising campaigns. Investors subscribe to new positions during the defined fundraising cycle — up to 90 days.

Participants SECZ-registered issuers and onboarded investors
Mechanism Subscription-based — investors commit capital during fundraising window
Duration Maximum 90-day fundraising cycle
Minimum Investment USD 100
Available Structures Private Equity, Debt, Project-Based
Platform Fee 5% of total funds raised (charged to issuer)
IPS Levy 1% of funds raised — transferred to Protection Fund

Secondary Market

Position Transfer & Liquidity

The secondary market allows existing investors to list and transfer their positions to qualified buyers — providing a structured liquidity pathway for investments in an otherwise illiquid asset class.

Participants Registered Crowdaxe investors (both sellers and buyers)
Mechanism Seller lists position; qualified buyer acquires via structured settlement
Available Structures Private Equity, Debt, Project-Based
Eligibility Buyer must be a registered sophisticated investor
Transfer Process Platform-mediated with documented settlement
Pricing Negotiated between parties within platform framework

Market Flow

End-to-End Market Process

From issuer application through to secondary market position transfer — the complete investment lifecycle.

01

Issuer Application

SECZ-registered issuers apply to raise capital through the platform with mandatory documentation and due diligence review.

02

Primary Market

Approved issuers are introduced to the marketplace. Investors subscribe to new positions during the fundraising cycle.

03

Secondary Market

Existing investors may list positions for transfer. Qualified investors can acquire existing positions through structured settlement.

Capital at Risk. Investing in unlisted securities involves risks including illiquidity and potential total loss of capital. Seek independent advice where appropriate.
