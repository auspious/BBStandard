Investor Protection Scheme

A Dedicated Financial Backstop

The IPS establishes governance protocols, mandatory issuer due diligence, and a dedicated financial backstop for qualifying adverse events — designed to enhance investor confidence.

What is the IPS?

The Investor Protection Scheme (IPS) is a dedicated financial mechanism embedded in the Crowdaxe platform. It establishes governance protocols, mandatory issuer due diligence processes, and a financial backstop for qualifying adverse events. The scheme is designed to enhance investor confidence and promote the sustainable growth of the crowdfunding ecosystem in Zimbabwe.

Why It Exists

Crowdfunding investments in private companies are inherently high-risk. The IPS provides an additional layer of oversight that goes beyond standard regulatory compliance — positioning Crowdaxe as a platform that actively manages platform-level risk, not merely complies with it.

Important Notice

The IPS provides an additional layer of oversight but does not guarantee the return of invested capital. Investors should not rely on the IPS as the primary form of investment protection. Crowdfunding investments are inherently speculative and often illiquid, with the risk of total capital loss.

The 1% IPS Levy

The Protection Fund is capitalised by a 1% IPS levy applied to all funds raised through the platform. This levy is collected at the point of successful fundraising close and is immediately transferred to the segregated Protection Fund.

Segregated Accounts

All levy funds are held in a segregated account, maintained entirely separately from Crowdaxe Capital's operating accounts. This means the Protection Fund is not at risk from Crowdaxe's own operational or financial difficulties.

Independent Administration

The segregated fund is administered by an independent trustee with fiduciary responsibility. The trustee has no reporting line to Crowdaxe Capital management and operates under its own governance charter.

What Is Covered

The IPS provides compensation in predefined scenarios including proven material fraud by an issuer. Coverage terms, limits, and claims procedures are set out in the IPS Framework Document, available to registered investors upon onboarding.

What Is Not Covered

The IPS does not cover general investment losses, market risk, or issuer commercial failure. The IPS is not an insurance product. It is not designed to compensate investors for the inherent business risks associated with early-stage or growth-stage investment.

Claims Process

Claims are submitted to the independent trustee, who adjudicates in accordance with the IPS Framework Document. The trustee's determination is final. Full claims procedures are provided to registered investors in the IPS Framework Document.

Independent Trustee

The Protection Fund is governed by an independent trustee with full fiduciary responsibility for fund administration, claim adjudication, and periodic reporting. The trustee operates independently of Crowdaxe Capital management and is appointed under a separate governance charter.

Reporting

The trustee publishes periodic reports on the fund's balance, claims activity, and governance compliance. These reports are made available to all registered investors on the platform.

SECZ Oversight

The IPS framework operates within the SECZ regulatory sandbox. The Securities and Exchange Commission of Zimbabwe has oversight of the overall platform operations, including the Investor Protection Scheme's governance structure.

Capital at Risk. Investing in unlisted securities involves risks including illiquidity and potential total loss of capital. Seek independent advice where appropriate.
